02 What Services We Provide

Daltons AI provides three core AI-powered automation services to real estate enterprises operating in Singapore, Dubai, New York, Tokyo and Hong Kong:

  • AI Lead Autopilot — Automated lead generation, qualification and outreach using AI to scrape LinkedIn, Google Maps and property databases. Leads are scored by budget, intent and timeline and delivered to client CRM systems.
  • AI Market Intelligence — Real-time monitoring of property markets across 5 cities with daily pricing alerts, competitor tracking and AI-generated investment reports sent directly to clients.
  • AI Deal Operations — Automated proposal generation, contract drafting, compliance checks, follow-up sequences and e-signature workflows for real estate deal management.

In delivering these services, Daltons AI processes personal data both as a Controller (for our own clients and website visitors) and as a Processor (processing lead data on behalf of our clients).

07 Your Rights Under GDPR

As a data subject under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (2016/679) and the Dutch UAVG (Uitvoeringswet Algemene Verordening Gegevensbescherming), you have the following rights:

Right of Access (Art. 15)

Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you.

Right to Rectification (Art. 16)

Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

Right to Erasure (Art. 17)

Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten"), subject to legal retention obligations.

Right to Portability (Art. 20)

Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format (CSV or JSON).

Right to Object (Art. 21)

Object to processing based on legitimate interest, including direct marketing.

Right to Restriction (Art. 18)

Request that we limit how we process your data in certain circumstances.

Right to Withdraw Consent (Art. 7)

Withdraw marketing consent at any time without affecting prior processing.

Right Not to Be Profiled (Art. 22)

Not be subject to solely automated decisions with significant legal effects.

To exercise any right, email privacy@daltonsai.com with your name and request. We respond within 30 calendar days. Identity verification may be required. All requests are free of charge.

10 AI Processing Transparency

Daltons AI uses artificial intelligence extensively in delivering its services. In accordance with the EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689), which entered full application for prohibited practices in February 2025 and for high-risk systems from August 2026, we disclose the following:

  • Lead scoring: AI assigns a 0–100 qualification score to business leads based on publicly available data. This is classified as low-risk AI under the EU AI Act. Clients may request human review of any AI-scored lead.
  • Automated outreach: Email sequences are generated and sent by AI on behalf of clients. Recipients are always informed they may be interacting with an automated system upon request.
  • Market intelligence reports: AI-generated reports contain factual market data supplemented by AI analysis. Reports are labelled as AI-generated.
  • Contract drafting: AI-generated contracts are clearly marked as AI-assisted drafts requiring human legal review before signing.
  • We do not use AI for decisions that produce significant legal effects on individuals without human oversight.

11 Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures as required by GDPR Article 32, including:

  • All data in transit encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher

  • Employee data protection training and confidentiality obligations
  • Incident response procedures with defined escalation paths
  • Vendor security assessments for all sub-processors

12 Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, Daltons AI will:

  • Notify the Dutch Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach (GDPR Art. 33)
  • Notify affected data subjects without undue delay where the breach is likely to result in high risk (GDPR Art. 34)
  • Provide notification including: the nature of the breach, categories and approximate number of affected persons, likely consequences, and measures taken
  • For Singapore: notify the PDPC within 3 calendar days for notifiable data breaches under the PDPA
